How to improve your chances

CampaignCritic has identified key structural components that creators can tweak in their project's description to increase the chances of reaching their funding goal. Your campaign is given weighted scores to reflect how effectively it utilizes each structural component. The graph below displays the scores of your project compared to some of the most successful projects.

Aim to match or exceed the scores of the top projects but within reason. If a score is negative (i.e., a red bar is facing left), that structural component needs your attention!


What do these structural components mean?

Using more images helps create a vivid and more readable campaign—they can be photos of your project, people using it, infographics, or even stock photos. Consider grabbing attention with more bolded text or building excitement with more exclamation marks. In addition, having a longer project description in terms of word count, is an opportunity to flesh out your ideas but also indirectly conveys how serious you are about your project.


Strategically increasing hyperlinks

Instead of filling your campaign with random hyperlinks, use them to link to relevant websites, thus serving as a "bibliography" to support your ideas and to build credibility. If your project received any limelight from blogs or the media, make sure to link to that article!


What are innovation words?

Word choice is crucial to building a persuasive campaign. The world's best marketing teams use "innovation"-related words, such as revolutionary, breakthrough, beautiful, magical, gorgeous, amazing, incredible or awesome, to create buzz. Using these words effectively helps emphasize the value of your project.


Bonus tips from the analysis

In addition to the key structural components above, CampaignCritic identified the following ways to further improve your chances:

  • Be succinct! Articulate your message using as few words as possible. This also goes for sentences per paragraph—avoid walls of text.
  • Try to reiterate the pledges and their monetary value in the project's description. Reminding a potential backer how they can contribute is important.
  • Talk about stretch goals—your plans in case you vastly exceed your funding goal. Not only does this demonstrate vision, it communicates to backers that you plan to use their money in a concrete, methodical manner.
  • Don't forget to thank your readers and any backers. Expressing gratitude for your supporters never hurts!

Good luck!


*This app is only relevant for Kickstarters in the USA and doesn't take into account a project's aesthetics, feasiblility, usefulness, market demand or media buzz—just some aspects of a project's description that are relatively easy for creators to tweak.
